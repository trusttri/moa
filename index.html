What is Moa?

We, a research team at the University of Michigan, aim to build Moa, a software for consentfully connecting PhD students affected by faculty advisors’ mistreatment or abuse of power — so that they can collectively discuss and navigate their situations.

We are in the early stages of developing Moa. Your input matters.

We are interviewing PhD students in Human-Computer Interaction, Information, and Computer Science to make design decisions for Moa. Read more below to see if you'd be interested in participating! If you are, sign up here: [link]

  • The interview will take about an hour. You will be compensated $20 per hour for your time, either as a check or virtual gift card.
  • We will show an imaginary scenario that depicts problematic advising dynamics and use that to anchor interview questions. This is in order to not directly ask for participants’ experiences. (The lead author went through multiple advisor changes and wants to make sure the interview process feels safe.)
  • All data will be anonymized. We will also ask participants if they want to exclude any of their answers from our research data.
  • This study is exempt from ongoing IRB review at the University of Michigan (HUM00246731).
  • We have already interviewed PhD students at the University of Michigan, so we are looking for PhD students from other institutions (i.e., if you are a U-M PhD student, you are currently not eligible to participate).
  • For now, we aim to interview PhD students in CS or Information PhD programs.
